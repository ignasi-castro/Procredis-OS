The ultimate cold email checklist for 2026.

Here's what we run across every campaign that generated 3000+ opportunities:

1️⃣ Infrastructure
· 50 subdomains, 2 mailboxes each — 100 total
· SPF, DKIM, DMARC on every subdomain
· Every subdomain redirects to your main domain
· 2-3 weeks warmup before a single send
· Dedicated IPs — open and click tracking off


2️⃣ List Building
· TAM mapped across minimum 3 providers
· Waterfall enrichment: Apollo → Findymail → BetterContact → FullEnrich...
· Double-verified through BounceBan
· Bounce rate under 2% before anything goes out
· After testing 30+ databases — the winner depends on your ICP geography


3️⃣ Campaign Setup
· Signals identified before copy is written
· 15 copy variants built and ready to test
· Emails under 80 words, no links in the body
· Subject line: 2 words, lowercase, sounds internal
· 10-15% variation across sends minimum


4️⃣ Copywriting Formula
· Sentence 1: the signal or observation
· Sentence 2: sniff a problem, solve a problem
· Sentence 3: social proof with a real number
· Sentence 4: soft CTA 
· PS line: something that proves this was written for them


5️⃣ Follow Up
· New angle every single time
· Reframe the problem, add value, change the offer
· Touch entire TAM once a month minimum
· Never say "just circling back"

## Service 2: ABM (Account-Based Marketing)

This is how Clay operates his own ABM campaign (is not our client and our case study), but the logic is the same what changes is the signals you track for awarness scoring + the plays you do to convert them into the next stage


Clay went from $0-$100M ARR in less than 5 years.

A success story we all should pay attention to.

We’ve been silently perfecting ABM with some of our clients before opening to public.

Clay and Parabola were some of the ABM programs we studied closely.


So here’s the full breakdown of Clay’s ABM strategy that took them to $100M ARR:


1️⃣ Study past customers

Clay understood exactly who their best customers were:
· Talking with AEs and CSMs
· Deeply analysing closed won and closed lost
· Backtesting firmographics, technographics and fit signals

Output: a real ICP built on data, not hypothesis.


2️⃣ Map your TAL

Clay built a list of exactly 2,000 target companies using:
· Lookalike providers like Ocean.io
· General providers like their own Clay search


3️⃣ Clean, enrich and score every account

· Use ClayAgent to qualify accounts
 · Qualfiied
 · Not Qualified
 · Remove not qualified accounts

· Score with Clay formulas and AI logic into:
 · T1: Strong fit
 · T2: Good fit
 · T3: Okay fit

· Enriched contacts inside the company
 · Decision makers
 · Champions
 · Influencers

Grade A contacts at Tier 1 accounts = maximum priority.


4️⃣ Track every account across 4 awareness stages

This is where most ABM strategies fail they treat every account the same. 

Identified:
· Account is in the list but has shown no intent yet

Aware:
· 2+ contacts connected with founders on LinkedIn or
· 1+ engagement on social content or
· 1 website visit in the last 30 days or
· Attended a low-intent event or
· Intro call from investors

Interested:
· Created a workspace or
· Visited a high-intent page (pricing, university, enterprise) or
· Positive reply to outbound or
· Attended a high-intent event

Evaluating:
· Filled the contact sales form or
· Had a discovery meeting with a GTM Engineer


5️⃣ Run the right play at the right stage

You don't send the same message to everyone. Each stage gets a different play.

Identified → Aware:
· LinkedIn auto-connections with ICP profiles
· LinkedIn paid ads to build brand awareness
· Personalised landing pages when they visit

Aware → Interested:
· Signal-based outbound (triggered by their activity)
· Warm intros and invitations to high-intent events
· Retargeting ads with more specific messaging

Interested → Evaluating:
· Case study campaigns from similar companies
· Webinars focused on their exact problem
· Deeper outbound sequences with more value

Evaluating → Close:
· Executive dinners
· Referral-based campaigns
· Manual hunter prospecting by GTM Engineers

Demand gen owns Identified → Interested.
Sales owns Interested → Close.


Result:
1. Marketing and sales finally work on the same accounts
2. Sales focuses on the accounts that are more likely to buy right now (more revenue with the same effort)
