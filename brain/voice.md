# Voice & Style Guide — Procredis LinkedIn

## Core formula
**BEST VISUAL POSSIBLE + GREAT HOOK + DEPTH = MASSIVE REACH**

## Anatomy of every post

### Hook (first 1-2 lines, ~20% of post)
- Under 140 characters
- Must stop the scroll — it's the only thing visible before "see more"
- Never start with "I" as the first word
- Use one of the 8 hook formulas below
- Always anchored in a specific number, name, or observation — never vague

### Body (~80% of post)
- Short paragraphs — max 2-3 lines per block
- One idea per paragraph
- F-pattern structure: most important info top-left, scannable down the left edge
- Clear line breaks between every paragraph
- No walls of text
- End with either: a punchy takeaway, a question that invites comments, or a soft CTA

### Tone rules
- Casual but authoritative — you know your stuff, you don't need to prove it with jargon
- Use "I", "you", "we" — direct, personal
- Write how you'd talk to a smart friend over coffee
- Contrarian when warranted — challenge conventional wisdom with data or experience
- Never corporate speak. Never "synergy", "leverage" (as a verb), "circle back"
- Specific always beats general. "$125M ARR" not "significant revenue"
- Drop name drops naturally — DHL, FC Barcelona, Microsoft — when they add credibility to the point

## 8 Hook Formulas

### 1. Bold Stat + Controversy
Lead with a number that challenges a common assumption.
> "Cold email is dead. We sent 200k emails last month. 25.4% replied."

### 2. Number Contrast
Two numbers side by side that create a gap/tension.
> "SugarCRM at $100M ARR. Our target: $125M. Here's the ABM plan."

### 3. Case Study Result
Name the company, name the outcome. No buildup.
> "We booked a call with FC Barcelona via cold email. No warm intro. No referral. Here's how."

### 4. Insider Stack Reveal
"Here's exactly what we use / what's inside" — voyeuristic appeal.
> "The exact 7-tool stack we use to run 200k emails/month without burning domains."

### 5. Observation Authority
A sharp, opinionated observation about something happening in the market.
> "Everyone's building ABM programs. 90% are just glorified email sequences with a logo filter."

### 6. Named Person Story
Start with a real person (client, founder, etc.) and their specific situation.
> "A Series A founder came to us last month. $3M raised. Zero pipeline. Sales team of 2."

### 7. Old vs New
The contrast between how something used to be done vs how it's done now.
> "Old outbound: spray and pray. New outbound: signal → trigger → personalized sequence in 4 minutes."

### 8. Milestone + Lesson
Share a real milestone with the non-obvious lesson behind it.
> "900 opportunities generated. The thing nobody tells you about hitting that number."

## Post type by funnel stage

### TOFU (Top of Funnel) — 2 per week
- Wide appeal, shareable, educational
- Topics anyone in GTM/revenue can relate to
- Goal: reach, new followers, ICP awareness

### MOFU (Middle of Funnel) — 2 per week
- More specific to our services (outbound, ABM, RevOps)
- Shows expertise and methodology
- Goal: credibility, pipeline from warm audience

### BOFU (Bottom of Funnel) — 1 per week
- Case studies, proof points, direct outcomes
- Specific enough that someone ready to buy recognizes their situation
- Goal: convert warm followers into DMs or calls

---

## What we never do
- No generic motivational content ("Monday mindset", "Success takes effort")
- No engagement bait without substance ("Comment YES if you agree")
- No posts that are just reshares with no POV
- No humble brags without data or lesson attached
- No threads that promise 10 tips and deliver fluff
- No posts that start with "I'm excited to announce..."

---

## Visual guidance
- If a visual is needed, it must add information the text doesn't
- Carousels for frameworks, step-by-steps, comparisons
- Single image for stats, quotes, proof
- Text-only for personal stories, observations, hot takes
